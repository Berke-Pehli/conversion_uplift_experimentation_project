# Power BI Measures and KPI Notes

## 1. Purpose

This file defines the main KPIs, naming conventions, and metric logic for the Power BI dashboard.

It is meant to keep the dashboard consistent and make sure the metrics shown in Power BI match the logic already established in SQL.

The measures in this file are mostly conceptual because many of the exported CSVs are already summarized. In Power BI, some visuals can use the pre-aggregated values directly, while others may use simple DAX measures for display and filtering.

---

## 2. Main KPIs

The dashboard should focus on the following KPIs:

- Customer Count
- Visit Rate
- Conversion Rate
- Average Spend per Customer
- Average Spend per Converter
- Total Spend
- Absolute Visit Uplift
- Relative Visit Lift
- Absolute Conversion Uplift
- Relative Conversion Lift
- Absolute Spend Uplift

These KPIs are enough to tell the experiment story clearly without overloading the report.

---

## 3. KPI Definitions

### Customer Count
Number of customers in the selected segment or reporting group.

### Visit Rate
Share of customers who visited after assignment.

Formula:
visit rate = visits / customers

### Conversion Rate
Share of customers who converted after assignment.

Formula:
conversion rate = conversions / customers

### Average Spend per Customer
Average spend across all customers in the selected group.

Formula:
avg spend per customer = total spend / customers

### Average Spend per Converter
Average spend among converted customers only.

Formula:
avg spend per converter = total spend / converters

### Total Spend
Total spend generated by the selected group.

### Absolute Visit Uplift
Difference between campaign visit rate and control visit rate.

Formula:
campaign visit rate - control visit rate

### Relative Visit Lift
Visit uplift relative to control.

Formula:
(campaign visit rate - control visit rate) / control visit rate

### Absolute Conversion Uplift
Difference between campaign conversion rate and control conversion rate.

Formula:
campaign conversion rate - control conversion rate

### Relative Conversion Lift
Conversion uplift relative to control.

Formula:
(campaign conversion rate - control conversion rate) / control conversion rate

### Absolute Spend Uplift
Difference between campaign average spend per customer and control average spend per customer.

Formula:
campaign avg spend per customer - control avg spend per customer

## 7. DAX Guidance

Because most exported files are already aggregated, many dashboard visuals can use the columns directly.

Examples:
- `visit_rate`
- `conversion_rate`
- `avg_spend_per_customer`
- `absolute_visit_uplift`
- `relative_conversion_lift`

That means the dashboard does not need overly complex DAX to be useful.

Use DAX mainly for:
- simple totals
- filtered counts
- formatting helpers
- optional custom cards

This is the right level for the current project.

---

## 8. Formatting Recommendations

Suggested formatting:
- rates as percentages
- spend values as currency
- uplift metrics as percentages where appropriate
- total spend as currency with thousand separators

Examples:
- Visit Rate → 18.28%
- Conversion Rate → 1.25%
- Avg Spend / Customer → $1.42
- Total Spend → $30,311.69
- Rel Visit Lift → 72.13%

Consistent formatting is very important for readability.

---

## 9. Most Important Business KPIs

If the dashboard must emphasize only a few metrics, prioritize:

1. Conversion Rate
2. Average Spend per Customer
3. Absolute Conversion Uplift
4. Relative Conversion Lift
5. Total Spend

These are likely the most business-relevant metrics for decision making.

Visit Rate still matters, but conversion and spend are usually more important as outcome measures.

---

## 10. KPI Interpretation Notes

### High visit rate
Indicates the campaign is effective at driving site traffic.

### High conversion rate
Indicates the campaign is effective at driving purchases.

### High average spend per customer
Indicates the campaign is generating more value across the full targeted population.

### High average spend per converter
Indicates converted customers are spending more, but this should be interpreted together with conversion rate.

### Positive uplift
Indicates the campaign performed better than control.

### Strong relative lift
Indicates meaningful improvement over the no-email baseline.

---

## 11. Final Recommendation for Power BI Use

For this dashboard, use the summary CSVs as the main source for visuals and KPI cards.

Use `customer_experiment_detail.csv` mainly for:
- drilldown
- detailed filtering
- optional advanced visuals

This keeps the report easier to manage and aligns well with the current project level and workflow.
